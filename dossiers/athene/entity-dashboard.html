The structural headline: Athene Holding Ltd. ("AHL", Bermuda — the top Athene holding company) does not own its US insurance companies directly. The ownership chain runs Athene Holding Ltd. → Athene USA Corporation (Iowa holding company) → Athene Annuity Re Ltd. ("AARe" — a Bermuda reinsurer) → Athene Annuity and Life Company ("AAIA", the lead US insurer, which owns the rest of the US fleet) and the other Bermuda reinsurers (Athene Life Re Ltd., Athene Life Re International Ltd., Athene Annuity Re II Ltd.). So the Bermuda reinsurer AARe — not the better-known Athene Life Re — is the entity everything hangs from. Cited to AAIA's Schedule Y Part 1A (YE2025 statutory statement).

Verified findings

  • Ownership sandwich — the Bermuda reinsurer AARe sits between the US holdco and the entire US fleet. Every US policyholder dollar is in a company whose direct 100% parent is a Bermuda reinsurer.
  • AADE dissolved — absent from FY2025 EX-21 and Schedule Y; AAIA (61689) is now the sole lead. Only AADE RML, LLC survives as a trace.
  • NAIC group 4734 is bigger than Athene — same regulated group holds Aspen, Catalina runoff (Alea, SPARTA, NAICC, ProBuilders), Venerable and LifePoint entities.
  • Athene ↔ Venerable link — ALRe owns 13% of VA Capital (Venerable's parent).
  • ACRA split visible — ACRA 1A only 33% Athene-held; balance is third-party ADIP capital. ACRA Holding 2 is 100% ALRe.
  • Reconciliations passed — AHL EX-21 ⊆ APO EX-21 (45/45); Schedule Y ↔ EX-21 consistent on the insurance core.
