The structural headline: Athene Holding Ltd. ("AHL", Bermuda — the top Athene holding company) does not own its US insurance companies directly. The ownership chain runs Athene Holding Ltd. → Athene USA Corporation (Iowa holding company) → Athene Annuity Re Ltd. ("AARe" — a Bermuda reinsurer) → Athene Annuity and Life Company ("AAIA", the lead US insurer, which owns the rest of the US fleet) and the other Bermuda reinsurers (Athene Life Re Ltd., Athene Life Re International Ltd., Athene Annuity Re II Ltd.). So the Bermuda reinsurer AARe — not the better-known Athene Life Re — is the entity everything hangs from. Cited to AAIA's Schedule Y Part 1A (YE2025 statutory statement).
How to read this page: the diagram below is the machine; the numbered badges on it are sections of this page, in order. Each section decomposes exactly one part of the picture and foots to it: ① money in → ② reserves → ③ the offshore door → ④ the sidecars → ⑤ the assets + Apollo's take → ⑥ the cushion → ⑦ the engine. Every number is cited to its filed source.

The book more than doubled ($75B → $159B), and the growth routed through the least-checkable channels: private-letter paper 3.4× in two years (15.6% → 25.3% of the book); no-identifier paper 5.8×; and in 2025 publicly-rated holdings shrank $5.1B in absolute dollars while the book grew $27.9B. Benign reading: a deliberate, disclosed strategy shift into Apollo-originated private credit at stable quality. Adverse reading: the share of the book whose grades face a market check is collapsing — printed quality stays flat partly because ever more of it is graded in a channel that cannot contradict it.

Cohort test (the strict discriminator, run 1): of the 293 bonds PL-rated at YE2023 ($11.7B), 239 were still held at YE2025 — and their migration was mostly benign: $2.2B upgraded vs $0.3B downgraded, 193 unchanged. Recorded as such — clean is a valid answer. The caveats that keep it open: the tail is violent (one 2.A went straight to NAIC-6 — a 12-notch miss), the letters never graduate to public ratings ($7.9B of the $8.9B held cohort is still PL), $2.9B exited untested, and two years of a young book is a short film.

Coverage boundary, logged: YE2021 and YE2022 investment schedules are not public — Athene's IR site posts only the 40-page core+notes part (file names say "part 2 of 5"), and the Wayback Machine has no copy of the other parts. The film starts at 2023 unless another public door opens.
