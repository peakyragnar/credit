# Blue Owl (Kuvare) — findings ledger (Phase 1 cross-section)

1. **A different animal: Blue Owl owns the MANAGER, not the insurers.** What it bought: KAM (Kuvare Asset Management, 100%, July 2024, $842M) plus a $250M preferred in Kuvare UK Holdings marked $267M → $304M. The insurers are AUM clients; the credit risk lives with policyholders while the manager collects fees ($27.9M → $67.6M management fees in one year; $19.2B IG-credit AUM).

2. **The preferred marks itself.** Blue Owl's Kuvare stake is a Level-III fair-value position — the mark is the manager's own model on an illiquid instrument in a company it also manages assets for. The +$54M uplift since purchase is unverifiable publicly.

3. **The fifth Bermuda pivot, compressing like the other four.** Kuvare Life Re ECR coverage 207% (2023) → 190% (2024) → 176% (2025) — the lowest in the cross-section and still falling. Bermuda (KLR FCRs + audited FS, Kuvare Bermuda Re FS) is the ONLY public transparency window into the group.

4. **Capital circularity, flagged.** Kuvare Life Re holds $40M of GILICO surplus notes (6% due 2049, 5.5% due 2051) — group capital counted at the carrier is an asset of the group's own reinsurer.

5. **The weakest rating in the cross-section.** Lincoln Benefit Life: AM Best B++ with NEGATIVE outlook (2024). The three US carriers (Guaranty Income LA, United Life IA, Lincoln Benefit NE) publish no statutory statements; no Kuvare GAAP exists publicly.

6. **Model implication for the thesis.** If private-credit-insurance stress arrives, Blue Owl's exposure is fee revenue and a preferred, not policyholder liabilities — the purest test of whether the managers or the balance sheets carry the risk.
