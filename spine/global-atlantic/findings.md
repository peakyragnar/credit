But its US carriers post statutory statements WITHOUT CUSIP-level investment schedules (verification pages only) — the position-level drill (PL share, aging, concentration) cannot be replicated publicly. Coverage boundary, logged.

2. **The machine mirror:** KKR → GAFG Ltd (Bermuda) → four US carriers + GA Re (pivot) + GAAL + co-invest sidecars. First Allmerica: C&S $148M under $15.8B admitted assets — the thinnest cushion in either group's fleet; flagged.

3. **Both spread engines compressed, same shape.** GA underwriting margin: FY2023 1.72% → 1Q26 ~1.17% (derived; the Q1-26 supplement dropped the printed rates table). The repricing race is a sector story, measured identically at two companies.

4. **Both companies print GAAP losses under positive "operating" earnings.** GA FY2025: net loss to shareholder −$1,203M vs +$1,081M adjusted-operating pre-tax; 1Q25 alone −$1,024M; 1Q26 −$184M. Athene: 1Q26 −$1,973M to common vs +$719M SRE. The adjusted-vs-GAAP divergence is structural to the model, not idiosyncratic.

5. **The two-ruler quality gap: GA's book is junkier on the market's ruler, and its regulatory ruler flatters more.** YE2025 AFS book ($95.7B): below-IG = 5.6% by NAIC vs 10.0% by NRSRO — a 4.4pt flattering gap (Athene statutory below-IG 2.9%; its analog risk sits in the 25.3% PL channel instead). Also caught by the gates: GA re-rated ~$6.4B of "non-rated" paper into rated buckets between supplement vintages (totals invariant — bucket migration only; logged per-cell by the parser).
